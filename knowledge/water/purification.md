If water may be contaminated, bring it to a rolling boil for at least one full minute. At elevations above 2,000 meters, boil for at least three minutes. Let the water cool in a covered, clean container.

Boiling helps kill many disease-causing organisms, but it does not remove fuel, heavy metals, salt, or chemical contamination. If water smells like fuel, solvents, pesticides, or sewage, avoid it when any safer source exists.

If boiling is not possible, use a clean cloth to remove visible sediment, then use a known disinfectant method only when instructions and supplies are available. Mark treated water containers so untreated water is not confused with treated water.
