Clean hands before preparing food, eating, treating wounds, handling medicine, or touching clean water containers. Clean hands after using the toilet, handling waste, cleaning floodwater, or caring for someone sick.

If safe running water is unavailable, pour a small stream from a clean container over the hands while another person helps, or use a container with a small hole as a handwashing station. Use soap when available and scrub all surfaces of the hands.

Alcohol-based hand sanitizer can help when hands are not visibly dirty, but it does not remove mud, chemicals, sewage, or all germs. If hands are visibly dirty, wipe off dirt first and wash with the safest available water and soap.

Keep handwashing water away from drinking water containers and food surfaces. Dry hands with a clean towel or air dry rather than using a dirty shared cloth.
