title: Sanitation and Hygiene During Outage

Poor sanitation can cause illness quickly during a blackout, especially when water is limited. Separate drinking water, handwashing water, dirty water, trash, human waste, and food preparation areas as much as possible.

Wash hands before handling food, after using the toilet, after touching trash or floodwater, and before caring for wounds. If running water is unavailable, use a small amount of safe water poured from a container. Soap and friction matter; do not dip dirty hands into stored drinking water.

If toilets do not flush, keep human waste away from living, cooking, and water storage areas. Use a lined bucket or container only if necessary, cover waste after each use, and store it where people, animals, and floodwater cannot spread it. Wash or sanitize hands afterward.

Keep trash sealed and away from food. Food scraps attract insects and rodents. If collection is unavailable, separate sharp objects, contaminated waste, and food waste so people do not get cut or exposed while moving trash later.

Clean food preparation surfaces with the safest available method before eating. Use clean utensils when possible. If someone develops vomiting or diarrhea, isolate their waste and eating utensils, increase hand hygiene, and prioritize fluids to prevent dehydration.
