When outdoor air is smoky, dusty, or irritating, reduce the amount entering the shelter. Close windows and doors, block obvious drafts with damp towels or tape if safe, and avoid activities that add indoor smoke such as candles, frying, or burning fuel indoors.

Choose the cleanest indoor room available. Keep physical activity low because heavy breathing increases exposure. People with chest pain, severe shortness of breath, blue lips, confusion, or worsening breathing need urgent medical help when available.

If you have a suitable mask or respirator, use it according to its instructions and keep it dry. Cloth over the face may reduce large particles but is not reliable protection from dangerous gases or fine smoke.

Do not run generators, grills, charcoal, or fuel stoves indoors or near openings. Smoke precautions must not create carbon monoxide danger.
