Carbon monoxide is an invisible, odorless gas that can kill quickly. It can come from generators, charcoal grills, gas stoves, camp stoves, vehicles, fireplaces, and fuel-burning heaters. Do not run these in enclosed or partly enclosed spaces.

Keep generators and fuel-burning equipment outside and far from windows, doors, vents, garages, crawl spaces, and neighboring homes. Opening a window is not enough to make indoor generator or charcoal use safe.

Warning signs include headache, dizziness, nausea, weakness, confusion, chest pain, shortness of breath, sleepiness, or multiple people feeling sick at the same time. Pets may also become weak or unusually sleepy.

If carbon monoxide is suspected, move everyone to fresh air immediately. Do not stay inside to gather belongings. Call emergency services when possible. If someone is unconscious, not breathing normally, or has chest pain, treat it as life-threatening.

Battery-powered carbon monoxide alarms are valuable during outages. If an alarm sounds, leave the building first, then seek help. Do not re-enter until the source is found and the space is safe.
