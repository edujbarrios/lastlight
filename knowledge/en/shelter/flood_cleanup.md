Floodwater can contain sewage, fuel, chemicals, sharp objects, and hidden electrical hazards. Do not enter flooded rooms if power may still be connected. Avoid fast-moving water and do not assume shallow water is safe.

Wear protective clothing if cleanup is unavoidable: sturdy shoes, gloves, long sleeves, and eye protection if available. Wash hands and exposed skin after contact with floodwater or contaminated mud.

Discard food, medicine, and hygiene items touched by floodwater unless official guidance says they can be safely cleaned. Porous items that stay wet can grow mold. Dry cleanable items quickly and increase ventilation when it is safe to do so.

If water smells like fuel, solvents, or chemicals, avoid skin contact and do not try to treat it for drinking. Seek a safer water source.
