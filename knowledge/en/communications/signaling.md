Signals for rescuers should be simple, repeated, and easy to notice. Three repeated sounds, flashes, or marks are widely recognized as a distress pattern in many contexts.

Use a whistle, mirror, flashlight, bright fabric, smoke only when fire risk is controlled, or large ground symbols in open areas. Avoid using up phone battery for constant signaling unless rescuers are nearby.

Make signals from a safe location. Do not climb unstable structures or enter hazardous areas only to improve visibility.
