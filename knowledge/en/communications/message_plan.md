When battery and network access are limited, send short messages that carry the most important facts: who is safe, where you are, what you need, and what you plan to do next. Text messages may work when calls fail.

Choose one out-of-area contact to collect updates and share them with others. This reduces repeated calls and saves battery. Write important numbers on paper in case the phone dies.

Use scheduled check-in times instead of keeping the phone active all day. Turn off background apps, dim the screen, and keep the phone warm in cold weather. Save voice calls for urgent decisions or when text messages are not working.

Avoid forwarding unconfirmed warnings. Bad information can send people toward danger or waste scarce power and attention.
