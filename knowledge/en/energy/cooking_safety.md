Cook outdoors or in a very well-ventilated area when using charcoal, camp stoves, grills, or fuel burners. Never use charcoal grills, gas grills, camping stoves, or generators inside a home, tent, vehicle, garage, or enclosed room. Carbon monoxide can kill without warning.

Keep flames and hot stoves away from curtains, bedding, dry grass, paper, fuel containers, and children. Set cooking equipment on a stable, nonflammable surface. Keep water, sand, a lid, or a fire extinguisher nearby before lighting fuel.

Use the least fuel necessary. Plan meals before lighting a stove, cook foods together when safe, cover pots to reduce heat loss, and avoid long simmering if fuel is scarce. Do not leave a flame unattended.

If cooking indoors is unavoidable because of immediate danger outside, avoid open flame when possible and improve airflow with windows or doors. Stop cooking immediately if anyone develops headache, dizziness, nausea, weakness, confusion, or unusual sleepiness.

Let equipment cool fully before moving or storing it. Store fuel away from heat, sparks, living areas, and children. Clearly mark fuel containers so they are never confused with water or food.
