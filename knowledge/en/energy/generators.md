title: Generator Safety
language: en
tags:

Operate fuel generators outdoors only, far from doors, windows, vents, garages, and enclosed spaces. Carbon monoxide is invisible and odorless and can kill quickly.

Keep generators dry and protect them from rain without enclosing them. Let the engine cool before refueling. Store fuel in approved containers away from living areas and ignition sources.

Do not connect a generator directly to household wiring unless a proper transfer switch has been installed by qualified personnel. Backfeeding can injure or kill utility workers and neighbors.
