A compass points toward magnetic north, not always true north. Keep it away from phones, speakers, magnets, vehicles, and metal tools while taking a bearing.

To follow a direction, hold the compass level, turn your body until the needle aligns with north, then move toward a visible landmark on your bearing. Recheck often.

When navigating with a map, account for local magnetic declination if known. In poor visibility, move slowly and use short legs between identifiable landmarks.
