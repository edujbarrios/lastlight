When water is limited, first separate safe drinking water from water for washing, cleaning, or flushing. Label containers clearly. Protect drinking water from dirty hands, open cups, and reused containers that held chemicals or fuel.

Do not ration water so severely that people become dehydrated. Reduce water loss instead: rest in shade, avoid unnecessary exertion, stay out of wind and heat, and eat salty or very dry foods only when enough water is available.

Use lower-quality water for cleaning only when it will not touch food, wounds, medicine, or drinking containers. Reuse relatively clean rinse water for non-drinking tasks when safe. Keep a small reserve for urgent medicine needs.

Watch for dehydration: very dark urine, dizziness, confusion, dry mouth, rapid heartbeat, or inability to keep fluids down. These signs matter more than a fixed rationing schedule.
