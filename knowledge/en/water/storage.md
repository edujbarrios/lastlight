Store drinking water in clean, food-grade containers with tight lids. Keep containers closed, shaded, and away from fuel, chemicals, trash, and floodwater.

Do not touch the inside of caps or container mouths. Pour water instead of dipping cups into storage containers. If a container becomes contaminated, clean and disinfect it before reuse.

For planning, a common emergency baseline is at least one gallon, or about four liters, per person per day for drinking and basic hygiene. Hot weather, illness, pregnancy, nursing, and heavy work can require more.
