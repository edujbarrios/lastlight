title: Dehydration Prevention
language: en

Prevent dehydration by drinking small amounts regularly, resting during the hottest part of the day, and reducing unnecessary exertion. Dark urine, dizziness, dry mouth, confusion, and inability to sweat can be warning signs.

When sweating heavily, water alone may not be enough. Food, broth, or an oral rehydration solution can help replace salts. Do not ration water to the point that people stop drinking; dehydration can reduce judgment and physical ability quickly.
