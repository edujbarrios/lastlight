Mild dehydration may improve with rest in shade and frequent small sips of safe water or oral rehydration solution. If the person vomits, wait a few minutes and resume with very small sips.

Warning signs include confusion, fainting, very little urine, rapid heartbeat, sunken eyes, inability to keep fluids down, or worsening weakness. These signs require urgent medical help when available.

For infants, older adults, and people with chronic illness, dehydration can become dangerous quickly. Monitor closely and prioritize safe fluids.
