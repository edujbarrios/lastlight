Cool a burn with clean, cool running water for about 20 minutes if possible. Remove jewelry or tight clothing near the burn before swelling starts, but do not remove material stuck to the skin.

Cover the burn with a clean, dry, non-stick dressing or cloth. Do not apply butter, oil, toothpaste, or powders. Do not break blisters.

Seek urgent care for burns that are deep, large, on the face, hands, feet, genitals, or major joints, or caused by chemicals, electricity, smoke inhalation, or explosion.
