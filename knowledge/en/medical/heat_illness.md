Heat illness can become life-threatening quickly. Warning signs include heavy sweating, weakness, dizziness, headache, nausea, muscle cramps, fast pulse, confusion, fainting, or hot dry skin.

Move the person to shade or the coolest available place. Loosen extra clothing. Cool the body with wet cloths, fanning, misting, or cool water on the neck, armpits, and groin. Give small sips of water if the person is awake, able to swallow, and not vomiting.

Confusion, fainting, seizure, very high body temperature, or worsening symptoms are emergency signs. Cool first while seeking urgent medical help. Do not give alcohol. Do not force fluids into someone who is confused, unconscious, or unable to swallow safely.

Prevent heat illness by resting during the hottest hours, reducing work intensity, wearing light loose clothing, and checking on people who may not notice early symptoms.
