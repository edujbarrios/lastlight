Hypothermia can occur when the body loses heat faster than it can produce it. Warning signs include intense shivering, clumsiness, confusion, slurred speech, drowsiness, and weak pulse.

Move the person out of wind and wet conditions. Remove wet clothing if dry coverings are available. Warm the core first with blankets, dry layers, body heat, or warm packs wrapped in cloth placed near the chest, neck, or groin.

Do not use direct high heat, alcohol, or rough handling. Severe hypothermia is life-threatening and needs urgent medical care.
