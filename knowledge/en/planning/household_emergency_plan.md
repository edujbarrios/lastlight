Make a simple household emergency plan before conditions get worse. Write down names, phone numbers, medical needs, meeting places, and out-of-area contacts on paper. Keep a copy near the exit and another with important supplies.

Choose two meeting places: one near the home and one outside the neighborhood. If people are separated and phones do not work, the plan should say where to go, who to check on, and how long to wait before moving to the backup location.

Assign practical roles when possible: who carries medicine, who turns off unsafe appliances if there is time, who brings documents, and who checks on people who need help. Keep the plan short enough to follow under stress.

Review the plan when conditions change. A safe plan during a blackout may not be safe during flooding, fire, chemical smoke, or evacuation orders. Follow official instructions when they are available.
