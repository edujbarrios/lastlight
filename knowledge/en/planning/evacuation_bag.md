An evacuation bag should help you leave quickly without depending on power, internet, or open stores. Keep it light enough to carry. Pack water, ready-to-eat food, needed medicine, copies of important documents, a flashlight, spare batteries, a phone charging cable, hygiene supplies, a small first aid kit, cash, and weather-appropriate clothing.

Store documents in a sealed bag: identification, medication lists, insurance details, emergency contacts, and any instructions needed for medical equipment. Paper copies matter when phones are dead or networks are overloaded.

Check the bag regularly. Replace expired food, rotate water, update medicine lists, and charge battery banks. Keep shoes, keys, glasses, and critical medicine in predictable places so they are not forgotten during a rushed departure.

If evacuation is ordered, leave early when you can. Roads, fuel, public transport, and visibility can get worse quickly.
