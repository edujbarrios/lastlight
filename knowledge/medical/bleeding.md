---
title: Severe Bleeding
language: en
tags:
  - medical
  - bleeding
  - pressure
priority: critical
---

For severe bleeding, apply firm direct pressure with clean cloth, gauze, or the cleanest available material. Keep pressure steady. Do not repeatedly lift the dressing to check the wound because that can restart bleeding.

If blood soaks through, add more material on top and continue pressure. If the bleeding is on an arm or leg and cannot be controlled with direct pressure, a tourniquet may be needed if one is available and the situation is life-threatening.

Seek emergency medical care as soon as possible. Watch for shock: pale or clammy skin, weakness, rapid breathing, confusion, or loss of consciousness. Keep the person still and warm.
